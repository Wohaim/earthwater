MACHINE LEARNING & NANO TECHNOLOGY FOR WATER LINKED SYSTEMS

AT SCALE

We apply sensing and machine learning to measure, discover, and predict, for water linked systems, and then apply nano technology to filter contaminated water at revolutionary extremely low energy technique, to produce clean water rapidly

Two Unique Approaches:

Optimising Water Linked Systems with High-Throughput Data and Machine Learning

The level of surface water runoff is crucial for understanding and optimizing downstream usage and balance of such scarce resource.

However, a lack of abundant and near real data during precipitation and runoff,limits our understanding and ability to optimizes linked water systems, such as hydropower plants that support the grid, water treatment facilities that treat storm water,flood prevention and prediction, and much more.

Earth Water generates and analyses big data needed to to discover and optimize downstream usage of surface runoff during precipitation events, using machine-learning models.

Increasing Clean Water Access through Modular Decentralized approach with increased efficiency using nano technology

Treating water in a decentralized structure, not only reduce the burden on current treatment facilities and technologies but also reduce the turnaround time for clean fresh water. Conversely, downstream wastewater treatment has to go through several stages, requiring an abundant of energy and chemicals slowing down the rate at which water is available. Our decentralized modular infrastructure, working at the nano scale, is a single stage treatment.

Our Technology

MEASURING AND TREATING WATER AT LARGE SCALE

HydroWink is the leading sensor platform for quantitative characterization of surface water runoff that feed to downstream Hydro systems. Leveraging artificial intelligence, we are aiming to generate the largest repository of machine learning models, that we can train, capable of predicting and forecasting, exceedance, using low-cost fusion sensor technology.

HydroLynk harnesses our accumulated data to train machine learning models capable of predicting binding from protein sequence. Computational designs are measured experimentally for model validation and refinement through rapid iterations.
