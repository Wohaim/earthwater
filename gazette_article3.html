Virginia Tech Center Research: Stratup Engine

VTCR to host first of many HBCU cohort for brining NASA'S technology to earth.

Earth Water System Inc., is honoroud to be at Tech Center Research Park to discuss innovation and commecialization of new technologies. The Virginia Tech Corporate Research Center's Tech Center Research Park (TCRP) has announced a partnership with NASA to bring space-related technology to the market. The collaboration will combine the resources of NASA's Space Technology Mission Directorate Technology Transfer Expansion (T2X) and TCRP's Accelerator Network (AN) programs to introduce entrepreneurs to NASA's extensive technology portfolio.

The T2X program aims to accelerate the commercialization of NASA technology-derived start-ups and encourages the development of new applications for technologies originally designed for the space program. Meanwhile, the AN program targets a diverse group of entrepreneurs, such as women and minorities, and partners with Historically Black College/Universities Network and other corporate partners to facilitate investor readiness. Under the collaboration, the AN program will promote the available use of technologies to under-represented start-up entrepreneurs, identify terrestrial applications, and evaluate licensing and commercialization potential. The program will begin on August 9, 2021, at TCRP in Newport News, VA.
